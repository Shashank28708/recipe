Dahi Vada

Ingredients :

(1) 1 cup Urad Dal (split black lentils)
(2) 2 cups fresh, thick Curd (yogurt)
(3) Green Chutney (as needed)
(4) Tamarind Chutney (as needed)
(5) Chaat Masala (to taste)
(6) Red Chili Powder (to taste)
(7) Roasted Cumin Powder (to taste)
(8) Salt (to taste)
(9) Oil (for frying)

Instructions :

Soak the Urad Dal: Soak the urad dal in water overnight or for at least 4-5 hours. Prepare the Batter: Drain the soaked dal and grind it into a smooth batter, adding a little water if necessary. The batter should be thick and fluffy. Fry the Vadas: Heat oil in a deep pan. Wet your hands and take a small portion of the batter, shape it into a ball, and gently drop it into the hot oil. Fry until golden brown. Repeat with the remaining batter. Soak the Vadas: Once fried, soak the vadas in warm water for about 20-30 minutes. Then gently press each vada between your palms to remove excess water. Whisk the Curd: Beat the curd until smooth. You can add a little milk or water to adjust the consistency if it’s too thick. Assemble the Dahi Vada: Place the soaked vadas in a serving dish. Pour the whisked curd over the vadas, ensuring they are fully covered. Add Toppings: Drizzle green chutney and tamarind chutney over the curd-covered vadas. Sprinkle chaat masala, red chili powder, and roasted cumin powder on top. Garnish: Garnish with chopped coriander leaves and pomegranate arils if available. Serve: Allow the Dahi Vada to chill in the refrigerator for an hour before serving.
Bhindi masala

Ingredients :

(1) 250 grams Bhindi
(2) 3 tablespoons Oil
(3) ½ teaspoon Cumin Seeds (jeera)
(4) 1 cup Onions (finely chopped)
(5) 1 Green Chilli (slit, optional)
(6) ¾ tablespoon Ginger Garlic Paste
(7) 1 cup Tomatoes (chopped or pureed)
(8) Salt (to taste)
(9) ¼ teaspoon Turmeric Powder (haldi)
(10) 1 teaspoon Red Chili Powder
(11) 1 teaspoon Garam Masala
(13) ½ teaspoon Kasuri Methi 
(14) 2 tablespoons Coriander Leaves

Instructions :

Prepare the Bhindi: Wash the bhindi and pat dry. Cut into 1-inch pieces. Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a pan and sauté the bhindi until they are cooked and no longer sticky. Remove and set aside. Make the Masala: In the same pan, add the remaining oil and cumin seeds. Once they splutter, add onions and green chili. Sauté until the onions turn golden brown. Add ginger garlic paste and sauté for a minute. Mix in the tomatoes, salt, turmeric, red chili powder, and coriander powder. Cook until the tomatoes are soft and the oil starts to separate. Combine Bhindi with Masala: Add the cooked bhindi to the masala and stir well. Sprinkle garam masala and kasuri methi over the bhindi. Mix gently and cook for another 2-3 minutes. Garnish and Serve: Garnish with chopped coriander leaves. Serve hot with roti, naan, or rice.
Achari Paneer

Ingredients :

(1) 16-18 Kashmiri Dry Red Chillies
(2) 2 tablespoons Coriander Seeds
(3) 2 teaspoons Onion Seeds
(4) 6 tablespoons Fennel Seeds
(5) 4 tablespoons Mustard Seeds
(6) Paneer (cottage cheese)
(7) Onions
(8) Curd (yogurt)
(9) Spices: Turmeric, Red Chili Powder, Garam Masala
(10) Mustard Oil
(11) Plain Flour (optional)

Instructions :

Prepare Achari Masala: Roast the Kashmiri dry red chilies, coriander seeds, onion seeds, fennel seeds, and mustard seeds. Grind them into a semi-fine powder. Sauté Onions: Heat mustard oil in a pan. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown. Add Achari Masala: Add the prepared achari masala to the onions. Mix well and cook for a minute. Add Curd and Spices: Whisk curd and add it to the masala. Season with turmeric, red chili powder, and garam masala. Cook the Gravy: Cook the gravy until the oil separates. If using plain flour, mix it with water and add to the gravy to prevent curdling. Add Paneer: Add cubed paneer to the gravy. Simmer for a few minutes until the paneer absorbs the flavors. Garnish and Serve: Garnish with fresh coriander leaves. Serve hot with rotis or butter naan.
Chhole Bhature

Ingredients :

(1) 1 cup dried white chickpeas (soaked overnight)
(2) 2 tablespoons oil
(3) 1 onion (finely chopped)
(4) 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
(5) Spices: Chole masala, chili powder
(6) dried mango powder, turmeric, garam masala
(7) Tomato puree, coriander powder, cumin powder
(8) Coriander leaves for garnish
(9) Maida, semolina (sooji), yogurt, baking soda, and oil

Instructions :

Soak the Chickpeas: Begin by soaking 1 cup of dried white chickpeas overnight. If you’re short on time, you can use canned chickpeas as a quicker alternative. Cook the Chickpeas: Drain the soaked chickpeas and cook them in a pressure cooker with water and a couple of tea bags. The tea bags are optional but they give the chickpeas a nice color. Cook until the chickpeas are tender. Prepare the Masala: In a pan, heat some oil and sauté finely chopped onions until golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a few minutes. Then, add the spices - chole masala, chili powder, dried mango powder, turmeric, coriander powder, cumin powder, and garam masala. Cook until the oil starts to separate from the masala. Combine and Simmer: Add the cooked chickpeas to the masala along with some tomato puree. Simmer the mixture for a while so that the chickpeas absorb all the flavors. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves before serving.
Palak Paneer

Ingredients :

(1) 150 grams paneer (Indian cottage cheese)
(2) 4 cups spinach (palak)
(3) 2 tablespoons oil (or a mix of oil and butter)
(4) 2 green chilies (deseeded for less spiciness)
(5) ¾ cup finely chopped onions
(6) ½ cup tomatoes (deseeded and chopped or pureed)
(7) ¾ teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
(8) ½ teaspoon salt (adjust to taste)
(9) 10 cashew nuts
(10) ¾ teaspoon garam masala
(11) ¼ cup water (to blend spinach,to cook the gravy)
(12) 3 tablespoons cream (optional)

Instructions :

Prepare the Spinach: Clean the Spinach: Rinse fresh spinach leaves thoroughly in water to remove any dirt or debris. Blanch the Spinach: Boil water in a pot, add the spinach leaves, and let them sit in the water for 2-3 minutes. This helps retain the vibrant green color. Ice Bath: After blanching, transfer the spinach to a bowl of ice-cold water. This stops the cooking process and keeps the color intact. Puree the Spinach: Drain the spinach and blend it into a smooth puree using a blender. Set aside. Make the Gravy: Saute Aromatics: In a pan, heat some oil or ghee. Add cumin seeds, asafoetida (hing), finely chopped onions, and saute until they turn translucent. Add Spices: Include ginger-garlic paste, green chilies, and other spices like turmeric and red chili powder. Cook until the raw smell disappears. Tomatoes: Add finely chopped or pureed tomatoes and cook until the oil separates from the mixture. Spinach Puree: Introduce the spinach puree to the pan, add some water to adjust the consistency, and let it simmer for a few minutes. Add the Paneer: Paneer Cubes: Cut the paneer into cubes or triangles as per your preference. Simmer with Gravy: Add the paneer to the gravy and let it cook for a few minutes so that the paneer absorbs the flavors.
Dosa

Ingredients :

(1) ½ cup urad dal 
(2) 2 cups cold filtered water (to blend dal)
(3) 3 cups rice (regular raw rice or basmati rice)
(4) 1 cup cold filtered water 
(5) 2 tablespoons chana dal (bengal gram)
(6) ½ teaspoon fenugreek seeds (methi seeds)
(7) 2 tablespoons poha (flattened rice)
(8) 1 teaspoon rock salt (crystal salt)

Instructions :

Step 1: Preparing the Batter Soaking: Soak ½ cup urad dal, 3 cups rice, 2 tablespoons chana dal, and ½ teaspoon fenugreek seeds in separate bowls for at least 4-6 hours. Soak 2 tablespoons poha in water for about 30 minutes before you’re ready to grind the batter. Grinding: Drain the water from the urad dal and grind it in a wet grinder or a high-power blender, adding 2 cups of cold filtered water gradually until you get a smooth and fluffy batter. Similarly, drain the rice, chana dal, and fenugreek seeds, and grind them together with 1 cup of cold filtered water (you may need 2 tablespoons more) to a fine consistency. Combine the ground poha with the rice mixture during the last few minutes of grinding. Mixing: Mix the urad dal batter and rice batter together in a large bowl. Add 1 teaspoon of rock salt and mix well. Step 2: Making the Dosa Preparing the Pan: Heat a cast-iron pan or non-stick skillet (tawa) on medium heat. Sprinkle a few drops of water to check if the pan is hot enough; the water should sizzle and evaporate quickly. Pouring the Batter: Stir the fermented batter gently. Pour a ladleful of batter in the center of the pan. Spreading the Batter: Quickly spread the batter in a circular motion using the back of the ladle, starting from the center and moving outwards, to create a thin crepe.
Idli

Ingredients :

(1) Urad dal (hulled black gram): Used for the lentil component.
(2) Rice: Traditionally, idli rice or parboiled rice is used.
(3) Salt: To season the batter.
(4) Water: For soaking and grinding.
(5) Baking soda (optional): Helps in fermentation.

Instructions :

Soaking: Rinse the urad dal and rice separately. Soak them in water for about 5 hours. Grinding: Drain the soaked urad dal and grind it to a smooth paste using water. Similarly, grind the rice to a slightly coarse consistency. Mix the ground urad dal and rice together. Seasoning: Add salt to the batter and mix well. Fermentation: Allow the batter to ferment in a warm place for 6-8 hours or overnight. The batter will increase in volume. Steaming: Grease the idli molds with oil. Pour the batter into the molds. Steam the idlis in an idli steamer or a regular steamer for about 10-12 minutes. Serve: Serve the soft idlis hot with sambar and coconut chutney.
Vada

Ingredients :

(1) 1 cup Urad Dal,3 cups Water
(2) 4 tablespoons Water (for grinding)
(3) ½ teaspoon Cumin Seeds
(4) ½ teaspoon Black Peppercorns (whole or crushed)
(5) 1 tablespoon Curry Leaves (chopped)
(6) ⅓ cup Onions (finely chopped, optional)
(7) 1 teaspoon Green Chilies (chopped)
(8) 1 teaspoon Ginger (finely chopped)
(9) A pinch of Asafoetida (hing, optional)
(10) ¼ cup Coconut Pieces , Oil (for deep frying)
(11) 2 tablespoons Coriander Leaves , ½ teaspoon Salt

Instructions :

Soak the urad dal in water for at least 4 hours. Drain the water and grind the dal to a smooth batter, adding very little water if necessary. To the batter, add cumin seeds, black peppercorns, curry leaves, onions, green chilies, ginger, asafoetida, coconut pieces, coriander leaves, and salt. Mix well. Heat oil in a deep frying pan. Wet your hands and take a small portion of the batter. Flatten it and make a hole in the center to give it a doughnut shape. Carefully drop it into the hot oil and fry until golden brown. Serve hot with coconut chutney or sambar.
Coconut Rice

Ingredients :

(1) 2 cups Jasmine Rice
(2) 400 milliliters Coconut Milk (full fat or light)
(3) 1 cup Water
(4) 5 teaspoons White Sugar
(5) ½ teaspoon Kosher Salt
(6) 1 Pandan Leaf (knotted)
(7) 2 Kaffir Lime Leaves (crushed)
(8) 1 tablespoon Toasted Desiccated Coconut (for garnish)

Instructions :

Rinse the Rice: Start by thoroughly rinsing the jasmine rice under cold water until the water runs clear. This step removes excess starch and prevents the rice from becoming too sticky. Cook the Rice: In a medium saucepan, combine the rinsed rice, coconut milk, water, salt, and sugar. Stir everything together and bring the mixture to a boil over medium-high heat. Simmer: Once boiling, reduce the heat to low, cover the saucepan with a lid, and let the rice simmer. This allows the rice to absorb the flavors and cook evenly. Check the Rice: After about 18-20 minutes, check the rice. It should be tender and all the liquid should be absorbed. If it’s not quite there yet, cover and cook for a few more minutes. Fluff and Serve: When the rice is done, turn off the heat and let it sit covered for 5 minutes. Then, fluff it with a fork to separate the grains gently. Sprinkle with chopped cilantro before serving, if desired.
Sambar

Ingredients :

(1) ½ cup tuvar dal (pigeon pea lentils)
(2) Mixed vegetables 
(3) Tamarind pulp
(4) Sambar powder (homemade or store-bought)
(5) Salt to taste

Instructions :

Cook ¾ cup of toor dal (split pigeon peas) in a pressure cooker until mushy. Boil vegetables like drumsticks, okra, and pumpkin until tender. Soak tamarind in water, extract juice. Combine cooked dal, vegetables, tamarind juice, and sambar powder in a pot. Simmer for 10-15 minutes; add salt to taste. Prepare tempering: heat oil, add mustard seeds, cumin, asafoetida, and curry leaves; pour over sambar. Garnish with coriander leaves and serve with rice or idli. Enjoy your homemade sambar with this simple and quick recipe! 🍲

Chow Mein

Basundi

Ingredients :

(1) ½ can sweetened condensed milk
(2) 2 cups whole milk
(3) ½ teaspoon cardamom powder 
(4) 6 cashews (slivered or chopped)
(5) 10 pistachios (slivered or chopped, unsalted)
(6) 8 almonds (slivered or chopped)
(7) 12 saffron strands
(8) 3 pinches of grated nutmeg or nutmeg powder
(9) 2 tablespoons chironji 
(10) 2 tablespoons almond slivers
(11) 2 tablespoons pistachio slivers

Instructions

In a heavy skillet or thick-bottomed saucepan, combine the sweetened condensed milk and whole milk. Whisk well to ensure an even mixture. Simmer the mixture on low heat until it thickens slightly. Add cardamom powder, saffron strands, and grated nutmeg. Garnish with slivered nuts (cashews, pistachios, and almonds) and chironji seeds. Serve warm or chilled. Enjoy this aromatic and creamy dessert!
Modak

Ingredients :

(1) 1 cup rice flour (150 grams)
(2) ½ teaspoon poppy seeds 
(3) ¾ teaspoon ghee (or oil)
(4) ¼ teaspoon salt
(5) 1 cup fresh grated coconut (100 grams)
(6) 1 cup jaggery 
(7) 1 teaspoon cardamom powder 
(8) ¼ teaspoon grated nutmeg 
(9) 1 tablespoon rice flour

Instructions

Prepare the Filling: Heat a pan and add grated coconut and jaggery. Cook on low heat until the mixture thickens and comes together. Add cardamom powder and nutmeg. Mix well and set aside. Prepare the Rice Dough: Boil water with ghee and salt. Add rice flour and mix well to form a dough. Cover and let it cool slightly. Shape the Modak: Grease the modak mould with ghee. Take a portion of the dough and flatten it in the mould. Add the coconut-jaggery filling in the center. Seal the edges and remove excess dough. Steam the Modak: Steam the modak for about 10-12 minutes. Serve warm as prasad to Lord Ganesha!
Rosogulla

Ingredients :

(1) 1 liter milk (full fat or whole milk)
(2) 3 tablespoons lemon juice
(3) 1 ½ cups refined white sugar (1/4 cup optional)
(4) 3 green cardamom pods (or elaichi)
(5) ½ teaspoon rose water (optional)
(6) A pinch of saffron strands (for garnishing)

Instructions

Boil 1 liter of full-fat milk. Add 3 tablespoons of lemon juice to curdle the milk. Drain the chenna (cottage cheese) and rinse under cold water. Knead the chenna until it’s smooth and pliable. Make small balls from the chenna without any cracks. Prepare sugar syrup with 1 ½ cups sugar and 4 cups water. Add cardamom pods to the syrup for flavor. Boil the chenna balls in the syrup for about 10 minutes. Cover and cook for another 10 minutes on low heat. Turn off the heat and let them sit in the syrup. Add rose water for extra fragrance (optional). Transfer rasgullas to ice-cold water to maintain shape. Let them soak in the syrup for a few hours. Garnish with saffron strands before serving (optional). Serve chilled for the best taste. Enjoy your homemade Rasgullas!
Malpua

Ingredients :

(1) 1 ¼ cups all-purpose flour (or wheat flour)
(2) ½ teaspoon green cardamom powder
(3) 1 teaspoon fennel seeds
(4) 1 ½ cups milk (use more as needed)
(5) 4 tablespoons ghee (for frying)
(6) 3 tablespoons milk powder (optional, or replace with mawa or khoya)
(7) ½ cup organic sugar
(8) 2 teaspoons lemon juice (optional)

Instructions

Gather flour, cardamom, fennel seeds, milk powder, yogurt, water, and ghee. Combine 1 cup flour, ½ tsp cardamom, and 1 tsp fennel seeds in a bowl. Add 3 tbsp milk powder for richness (optional). Mix in 3 tbsp yogurt to bind the ingredients. Gradually pour in ½ cup water while stirring to create a smooth batter. Let the batter rest for 30 minutes to blend flavors. Heat ghee in a pan for frying. Pour spoonfuls of batter into the hot ghee. Fry until edges are crispy and the center is soft. Flip to cook both sides to a golden brown. Prepare syrup by dissolving sugar in boiling water. Add a touch of lemon juice to the syrup to prevent crystallization. Soak the fried malpuas in the warm syrup. Allow them to absorb the syrup for a few minutes. Serve the malpuas garnished with chopped nuts. Enjoy your delicious Malpuas!
